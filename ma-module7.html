Session 7.0

Understanding World Mission

God's Heart for All Nations

The Global Mandate

Missions is not an optional activity for a few; it is the collective responsibility of the Church. It involves establishing intentional relationships with the unreached so the Gospel can be communicated and demonstrated. From proclamation to transformation, our goal is to ensure every tribe and tongue has access to the Light of Christ.

The Mission Reality

3.2 Billion unreached worldwide.
81% of non-Christians have no personal contact with a believer.
379 Million unreached in Africa alone.
Only 20% of missionaries serve the unreached.

The Three Phases

Presence: Lifestyle demonstration. Proclamation: Verbal communication. Persuasion: Inviting a decision for Christ.

The Missionary Call

Whether as a full-time "sent one," a digital advocate, or a Kingdom financier, every believer has a functional role in the Great Commission.

The Urgency

Every hour, thousands enter eternity without ever hearing the Gospel. The reality of eternal separation (Luke 16) gives missions its gravity. A church’s commitment to sending and supporting is the true thermometer of its spiritual health.
