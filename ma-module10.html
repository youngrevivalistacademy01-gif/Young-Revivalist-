Session 10.0

Divine Healing

The Children's Bread — Matthew 15:26

Healing is Bread, Not a Bonus

Jesus identified healing as the "Children’s Bread"—a daily, essential nourishment that belongs to the household of God. You do not fast to convince God to give you bread; you receive it because it is your covenant right. Healing follows those who believe (Mark 16:17-18).

The Finished Provision

Isaiah 53:5 and 1 Peter 2:24 use the past tense: "By whose stripes you WERE healed." This reveals that healing is not a future hope but a finished work. While we live in a fallen world, sickness has no legal right to dominate the believer's body.

How to Encounter Healing

Know God's Will

Faith begins where the will of God is known. Feed on the Word until doubt loses its influence (Rom 10:17).

Cry in Faith

Approach the throne boldly (Heb 4:16). God responds to faith-filled dependence, not panic-driven desperation.

Act on Faith

Faith is expressed through action (James 1:22). Corresponding action demonstrates trust in God's Truth above symptoms.

Channels of Flow

  • The Word: Speak restorative power over sickness (Psalm 107:20).
  • The Prayer of Faith: Confident trust in the promise (James 5:15).
  • Praise: Focus on God's power to silence fear (Psalm 50:23).
