Session 5.0

Apologetics: Handling Objections

Reasoning with Gentleness & Respect

Defense Without Offense

Apologetics is not about winning arguments, but winning souls. Based on 1 Peter 3:15, we are called to give a reason for our hope with meekness. In this module, we dismantle the fear of difficult questions—such as the problem of evil, science vs. creation, and the reliability of scripture—learning to use these objections as bridges rather than barriers to the Gospel.

The Basic Attitude

Avoid hostility. Use positive reinforcement like "I'm glad you asked that" to diffuse tension and open the heart of the seeker.

Truth and Logic

Addressing the core "Why": From the existence of suffering and free will to the historical accuracy and manuscript support of the Bible.

Core Objections Handled

  • The Problem of Evil: Free will and the plan of redemption.
  • Science: Design vs. Chance and the complexity of DNA.
  • Pluralism: The unique, historical resurrection of Jesus.
  • Hypocrisy: Following Christ, not flawed humans.
